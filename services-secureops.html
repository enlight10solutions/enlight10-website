SecureOps Service Variants

The same foundation, tuned for three common commercial realities: Enterprise SOCs, MSSPs, and SMBs.

SecureOps for Enterprise

For in-house security teams that already own tools but need a more coherent SOC operating model.

  • 24/7 or business-hours monitoring and triage.
  • Case-driven investigations with impact and recommendations.
  • Integration with your existing SIEM, EDR, and ticketing stack.
  • Executive-ready reporting tied to risk and compliance needs.

SecureOps for MSSPs

For MSSPs and SOC providers that want a consistent way to manage many tenants using Watchtower CRP.

  • Multi-tenant patterns for cases, alerts, and playbooks.
  • Standardized triage, escalation, and reporting across clients.
  • Evidence and narratives you can show to your customers, not just ticket counts.
  • Optional white-label-style workflows with your brand and processes.

SecureOps Lite (SMB)

For SMBs and non-24/7 teams that need structure and evidence, not a full-blown SOC.

  • Right-sized alerting with clear priorities and action plans.
  • Business-hours monitoring with on-call or surge support patterns.
  • Incident timelines and “what we did” records for leadership and auditors.
  • On-ramps to CMMC and other frameworks without massive upfront investment.

Core SecureOps Capabilities

All SecureOps variants share a core set of capabilities — the difference is how far we scale them and how we shape them around your environment.

Monitoring & Triage

Eyes on glass, but smarter:

  • Alert triage and initial investigation inside Watchtower.
  • Enrichment from your existing tools (EDR, threat intel, identity).
  • Priority scoring so your team focuses on what matters most.

Investigation, Hunt & Response

Turn raw data into clear stories of what happened and what you did.

  • Atlas-powered cases with timelines and artifacts.
  • Campaign and user/host-centric investigations via Watchtower buckets.
  • Proactive hunts designed around your risk profile.

Reporting & Compliance Evidence

Operations that can stand in front of auditors, boards, and regulators.

  • Regular operational reporting (weekly, monthly, quarterly).
  • Evidence packs for audits (CMMC, ISO, SOC 2, internal standards).
  • “Here’s what we saw, here’s what we did, here’s what changed” narratives.

Powered by the Watchtower Cyber Reasoning Platform (CRP)

Watchtower SecureOps uses the CRP as the reasoning and evidence layer above your tools.

  • Guardian: organizes alerts and signals into triage queues and funnels.
  • Seek: fuses events into WCS-backed buckets that represent behaviors and campaigns, not just single alerts.
  • Atlas: the investigation workspace where analysts build and track cases, attach artifacts, and record decisions.
  • Assist: AI copilots to summarize cases, draft reports, and suggest next steps — always under analyst control.
  • Deploy: orchestrates playbooks for enrichment, ticketing, and automated containment where you’re comfortable.
  • Audit: organizes cases, timelines, and artifacts into reusable evidence across controls and frameworks.

You keep your existing tools. Watchtower CRP and SecureOps wrap them in a single operating picture.

How a SecureOps Engagement Works

  • 1. Discovery: understand your environment, tools, staffing, and regulatory pressures (CMMC, SOC 2, HIPAA, etc.).
  • 2. Design: define scope (networks, tenants, data sources), target outcomes, and reporting cadence.
  • 3. Watchtower Integration: connect your SIEM, EDR, identity, and other data sources into Watchtower CRP.
  • 4. Run SecureOps: operate monitoring, triage, investigations, and reporting using Watchtower as the central workspace.
  • 5. Refine & Scale: tune playbooks, automation, and coverage; expand to more assets, tenants, or business units over time.
